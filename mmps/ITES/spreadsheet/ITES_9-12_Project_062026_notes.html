Lesson 10: Final Practical Project

Core Concepts (Project Planning)

  • Application of Skills: The final project is designed to test your ability to integrate all the tools you have learned (formatting, formulas, functions, charts, and data validation) into a single, functional spreadsheet system.

  • Real-World Scenarios: Each option represents a standard business or personal management tool used in the real world.

  • Project Lifecycle: Moving from raw data entry to organized processing, and finally to professional, automated reporting.

Practical Project: The Capstone System

Objective: Design a complete, fully functional spreadsheet system based on one of the provided real-world scenarios.

Step 1: Choose Your Project Option (Select ONE)

  • School Fee Management System: Track student enrollments, fee categories, payments made, and outstanding balances.

  • Student Result Management System: Create a master grading sheet for multiple subjects, calculating totals, averages, letter grades, and passing status.

  • Shop Billing System: Design an invoice generator that calculates item costs, subtotals, tax, and final totals based on inventory data.

  • Personal Budget Planner: Build a comprehensive multi-month tracker comparing projected income and expenses against actual spending habits.

  • Library Record Management: Develop a database tracking book inventory, borrower details, issue dates, return dates, and late fee calculations.

Step 2: Meet the System Requirements Your final submission must include the following elements:

  • Proper Formatting: Use clear headers, borders, shading, conditional formatting, and appropriate alignments to make the data professional and easy to read.

  • Use of Formulas and Functions: Include automated calculations (e.g., SUM, AVERAGE, MAX, MIN, COUNT) relevant to your chosen topic to eliminate manual math.

  • Charts and Analysis: Provide at least one well-formatted chart (Pie, Bar, or Line) that visually summarizes key data from your project, complete with a title and labels.

  • Organized Worksheets: Use multiple, clearly named sheets (e.g., "Data Entry", "Calculations", "Summary Dashboard") to separate raw data from final reporting.

  • Print-Ready Layout: Ensure your final report or dashboard fits neatly on a standard printed page, with adjusted margins, correct orientation, and proper print areas set.

Key Skills Unlocked

  • Comprehensive spreadsheet architecture

  • Independent problem solving and logic application

  • Professional data presentation and reporting
